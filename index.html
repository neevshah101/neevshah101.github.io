Neev Shah

Hi! I’m a 4th year undergraduate at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER), Pune studying a mix of Physics, Astronomy and Data Science. I am broadly intereted in gravitational wave astronomy and cosmology, especially the things we can learn about our universe through the new window of gravitational waves and through multimessenger astronomy. I am also interested in computational astrophysics, simulations and using various ML tools to our benefit in furthering our understanding of the cosmos. Currently, I am working with Dr Susmita Adhikari on studying the prospects of core collapse in self interacting dark matter (SIDM) halos. This summer, I am very excited to start working on my final year project with Dr Ajith and the ICTS Astrophysical Relativity group on gravitational lensing of gravitational waves. I have previously worked on detecting blackholes orbiting luminous companions in the Milky Way using TESS and also on using Random Forests, a ensemble learning method to distinguish between gravitational waves and glitches (transient noise) in the LIGO data

Outside of research, I love trekking and exploring new places. In 2023, I am also trying to pick up reading as a hobby. In the last 2 months I have read Never Let Me Go by Kazuo Ishiguro, When Breath Becomes Air by Paul Kalanithi, The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo and Carrie Soto is Back by Taylor Jenkins Reid. I am currently reading The Palace of Illusions by Chitravkar Banerjee and next up in my list is Frankenstein by Mary Shelley.

I am an aspiring astrophysicist, and will be applying for a PhD later this year to start in Fall 2024.
